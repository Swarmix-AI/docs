# Tutorial: Invite Members & Assign Roles

**Audience:** Org Admin
**Format:** Screen recording (OBS)
**Duration:** ~2 minutes
**Prerequisites:** Organization and project created (Tutorial 01)

## Learning Objectives

1. Invite team members to the organization
2. Assign roles (admin vs member)
3. Assign members to projects

## Script

### Scene 1: Introduction (10 sec)

> Now that your organization is set up, let's bring your team on board and assign them to projects.

**Visual:** Organization dashboard.

### Scene 2: Inviting Members (45 sec)

> Members join via email invitation. You control who gets access and what role they play.

**Steps to show:**

1. Navigate to Organization Settings → Members
2. Click "Invite Member"
3. Enter email address (e.g., `alice@acme.dev`)
4. Select role: **Admin** (can manage org settings, members, billing) or **Member** (can use projects they're assigned to)
5. Click "Send Invitation"

**Visual:** Show the invitation form. Highlight the role selector with a callout explaining each role.

> Alice will receive an email with a link to join. Once she accepts, she'll appear in the members list.

### Scene 3: Assigning to Projects (30 sec)

> Members need to be assigned to projects to see conversations and participate.

**Steps to show:**

1. Click on a member in the members list
2. Click "Assign to Project"
3. Select the project (e.g., "Payment Gateway Rewrite")
4. Confirm

**Visual:** Show the project assignment dropdown.

> Members can be assigned to multiple projects. They'll only see conversations and data for their assigned projects.

### Scene 4: Managing Existing Members (20 sec)

> You can change roles, reassign projects, or remove members at any time from this same page.

**Steps to show:**

1. Show the member list with status indicators (active, pending invitation)
2. Click the menu on a member — show options: Change Role, Remove from Project, Remove from Org

### Scene 5: Wrap-up (15 sec)

> Your team is now set up. Members can log into the dashboard and start participating in conversations within their assigned projects.
