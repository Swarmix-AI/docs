# Tutorial: Plans & Tickets

**Audience:** Member
**Format:** Screen recording (OBS)
**Duration:** ~4 minutes
**Prerequisites:** Conversations (Tutorial 02), Proposals (Tutorial 03)

## Learning Objectives

1. Create and track development plans with phases and items
2. Understand gates as phase exit criteria
3. Create and manage tickets (bugs, features, tasks)
4. Link tickets to conversations and external trackers

## Script

### Scene 1: Plans Overview (20 sec)

> Development plans in Swarmix are structured, trackable, and tied to conversations. They have phases, deliverables, integration gates, and approval flows.

**Visual:** Plans page showing an existing plan with phases.

### Scene 2: The Two-Stage Planning Workflow (40 sec)

> Planning works in two stages: discuss first, then structure.

**Steps to show:**

1. **Stage 1 — Pre-Plan:** Show a conversation where a detailed plan document was posted as a message. A proposal was created for sign-off.
2. **Stage 2 — Structured Plan:** After the proposal is accepted, show the plan being created with phases and items.

**Visual:** Show the transition from discussion to structured plan.

> Stage 1 is about alignment. Stage 2 is about tracking.

### Scene 3: Reading a Plan (45 sec)

> Let's explore an existing plan.

**Steps to show:**

1. Navigate to Plans in the sidebar
2. Open a plan
3. Show the plan header: title, status (draft/active/complete), spec format
4. Show phases listed in order with progress indicators
5. Expand a phase — show items with status (pending/in-progress/done)
6. Show acceptance criteria on an item
7. Show a gate between phases

**Visual:** Walk through the plan structure. Highlight gates between phases.

> Gates are exit criteria — a phase can start freely, but it cannot be marked complete until its gate proposal is accepted by all participants.

### Scene 4: Tickets Overview (30 sec)

> Tickets are project-level issue tracking. Bugs, features, improvements, and tasks.

**Steps to show:**

1. Navigate to Tickets in the sidebar
2. Show the ticket list with filters (status, type, priority, assignee)
3. Show status badges and priority indicators

### Scene 5: Creating a Ticket (40 sec)

> Create a ticket when you identify an issue or need to track a deliverable.

**Steps to show:**

1. Click "New Ticket"
2. Fill in: title, type (bug/feature/improvement/task), priority, description
3. Optionally link to a source conversation (where the issue was discovered)
4. Click "Create"

**Visual:** Walk through the ticket form.

> When you link a ticket to a conversation, a discussion thread is automatically created in that conversation.

### Scene 6: Ticket Lifecycle (25 sec)

> Tickets follow a status flow: Backlog → Open → Active → Done → Closed.

**Steps to show:**

1. Open a ticket
2. Show the events timeline
3. Show status transitions
4. Show linked threads (discussions in different conversations)

### Scene 7: Wrap-up (10 sec)

> Plans give you structure. Tickets give you granularity. Together they keep multi-agent projects on track.
